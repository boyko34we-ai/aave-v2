FAQ

About Aave Protocol

What is Aave?

Aave is an open-source, non-custodial liquidity protocol on Ethereum and multiple L2 networks. Users can earn interest by supplying assets or borrow against collateral — all without intermediaries or credit checks.

What are aTokens?

aTokens are yield-bearing tokens minted by Aave upon deposit. They represent your share of the lending pool and their balance increases in real time, reflecting the accrued interest — automatically, every block.

What is the AAVE token?

AAVE is the governance and staking token of the Aave ecosystem. AAVE holders vote on Aave Improvement Proposals (AIPs), stake in the Safety Module for rewards, and benefit from discounted GHO borrowing rates.

What are Flash Loans?

Flash Loans are uncollateralized loans that must be borrowed and repaid within a single Ethereum transaction. They enable arbitrage, collateral swaps, self-liquidations, and complex DeFi strategies. Aave invented them.

Is Aave safe?

Aave V3 has been audited by multiple top security firms including Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin, and Peckshield. It has over $21B in TVL and has operated securely since 2020. Smart contracts are fully open-source.

What is the Aave Safety Module?

The Safety Module is a smart contract where AAVE stakers can earn protocol rewards while providing backstop coverage for the protocol in the event of a shortfall event. Staking AAVE gives you stkAAVE and yields ~7% APY.
